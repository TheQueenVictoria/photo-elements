Light

  • Front-lit: Light comes from the camera position. Few Shadows.
  • Side-lit: Light comes from one side, shadows are cast to the other.
  • Back-lit: Light comes toward the camera, front of subject is shaded.

Light Quality

  • Direct light: hard-edged, often dark shadows
  • Diffused: no, or almost no shadows.
  • Directional Diffused: Distinct but soft edged shadows
  • Silhouette: Subject dark and backlit, against a light background.
  • Glowing Light: Light comes from or seems to come from the subject

Tone & Contrast:

  • High Key: mostly light tones
  • Low Key: mostly dark tones
  • Full Scale: Many tones of black, gray and white.
  • High contract: Both very dark and very light areas, with few middle grays.
  • Low Contrast: Mostly middle grays

top

Texture

  • emphasized: usually due to light hitting the subject at an angle.
  • minimized: texture is missing usually due to front-lit, back-lit, or high-contrast.

top

Focus

  • sharp focus: everything in the photo is in focus
  • soft focus: nothing in the picture is in sharp focus
  • selective focus: the subject is in sharp focus and the rest is in soft focus

top

Depth of Field

  • shallow depth of field short distance between the nearest and farthest sharply focused areas.
  • extensive Depth of Field Large distance between the nearest and farthest sharply focused areas. Everything or Almost everything is in focus

Space & Perspective

  • shallow space: the foreground and background of the picture are close together.
  • deep space: the foreground and background are at great distances.
  • postive space: the most important element of the composition
  • negative space: empty space that surrounds the subject
